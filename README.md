## What is SBTI?

SBTI stands for **Silly Big Personality Test**. It borrows the structural idea of MBTI but throws out the academic seriousness entirely. The result? A set of personality types that feel oddly accurate — described in the language of internet culture, memes, and dry self-humor.

It's not a psychological tool. It's a mirror with a comedic filter. You answer 30 questions across 15 dimensions, and the test tells you which of the 27 types you are — complete with a name, a description, and a shareable result card.

## Why Did It Go Viral?

SBTI exploded on Weibo and Douyin almost overnight. The test server crashed within hours of launch due to overwhelming traffic. The reason it spread so fast is simple: the personality descriptions are short, punchy, and hit uncomfortably close to home. Labels like "DEAD" (existential exhaustion), "ATM-er" (always giving, never receiving), and "MALO" (just a small NPC in life's side quest) resonated deeply with young people navigating burnout, social pressure, and uncertainty.

The results are also inherently shareable — they're designed to be posted, compared, and laughed about with friends.
